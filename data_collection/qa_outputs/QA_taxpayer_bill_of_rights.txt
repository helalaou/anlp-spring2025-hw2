Q: What is the obligation of taxpayers according to the local taxpayer's bill of rights disclosure statement?
A: Taxpayers are obligated to file all local tax returns voluntarily and pay all local taxes to which they are subject.


Q: What rights does the local taxpayer's bill of rights provide taxpayers in Pittsburgh?
A: It grants legal rights in connection with audits, examinations, appeals, or refund claims of taxes and enforcement or collection actions by the city treasurer.


Q: Which taxes are considered eligible under the local taxpayer's bill of rights in Pittsburgh?
A: Eligible taxes include earned income tax, local services tax, business privilege tax, mercantile tax, mercantile license, amusement tax, net profit tax, wage tax, institution and service privilege tax, payroll expense tax, and parking tax.


Q: What must the Department of Finance do during an audit if they determine an underpayment exists?
A: They must notify the taxpayer in writing of the basis for the underpayment, including tax periods, amounts, legal basis, and itemization of revisions.


Q: How long does a taxpayer have to respond to requests for information during an audit?
A: A taxpayer has at least thirty (30) calendar days to respond, with possible extensions granted for good cause.

Q: When can lawful action be taken against a taxpayer for a specific tax year?
A: Lawful action cannot be taken until the expiration of the applicable response period, including extensions.


Q: What information can the finance department require from a taxpayer for tax enforcement or collection?
A: The finance department may require copies of the taxpayer's federal individual income tax return if it is reasonably necessary and not available from other sources.


Q: How long does a taxpayer have to file a petition for reassessment if they disagree with an assessment?
A: A taxpayer has 90 days from the date of the assessment notice to file a written petition for reassessment.


Q: What is the deadline for the finance department to issue a decision on a petition for reassessment?
A: Decisions on petitions shall be issued within sixty (60) days of receiving a complete and accurate petition.


Q: What is the timeframe for a taxpayer to request a refund or credit for an overpaid tax?
A: A request for refund or credit must be made within three (3) years of the due date for filing the report or one (1) year after actual payment of the tax, whichever is later.

Q: Where should written requests for payment issues be mailed in Pittsburgh?
A: Audit Section Treasurer – City of Pittsburgh, 414 Grant St, Pittsburgh, PA 15219-2476.


Q: How can a taxpayer file a complaint about an action the Finance Department has taken?
A: A taxpayer can submit a written complaint to the Treasurer at 414 Grant St, Pittsburgh, PA 15219-2476.


Q: What actions can the Finance Department take if a taxpayer has not paid a tax liability?
A: The Finance Department may inquire directly, audit records, contact the taxpayer, use a collection agency, issue a wage attachment, deny licenses or permits, file a civil lawsuit, or file a criminal complaint.


Q: What might the Finance Department do if a taxpayer does not remit timely payment of taxes?
A: They may issue a wage attachment to the taxpayer’s employer.


Q: What consequence might a taxpayer face for not filing a properly completed tax return?
A: The Finance Department may deny the issuance of any applicable license or permit.
